Project

AI-Powered Assistant for Customer Service Teams

Turning high-volume conversations into structured, actionable knowledge while reducing manual effort.

Role: Senior UX/UI Duration: 10 semanas Team: PM • Eng • Ops
Overview

Contexto, problema y objetivos

Takeaway: A European insurer aims to turn daily conversations into structured, actionable knowledge—reducing manual entry, standardizing documentation and activating insights, without disrupting agent workflows.

View more

Context of the project

A European insurance company is exploring AI-driven solutions to improve internal operations in customer service. It manages thousands of daily interactions via phone and chat with policyholders and prospects.

These conversations are key to resolving issues, handling claims, and building trust, yet the processes around them remain manual, slow, and inconsistent.

The call center carries high operational costs but also holds untapped potential—each interaction contains valuable insights that aren’t fully leveraged.

Initial Problem Assessment

Agents manually enter data during/after calls, leading to errors, inconsistent records, and reduced productivity.

Valuable call data goes unanalyzed, missing opportunities to improve case tracking, customer experience, and internal processes.

Business Goals

  • Operational Efficiency: Automate during and after calls to reduce idle time and agent workload.
  • Data Quality & Consistency: Standardize post-call documentation and integrate with CRM for reliable tracking and decisions.
  • Insight Activation: Detect patterns, sentiment, and key topics to inform product, process, and service improvements.

Design Focus

We defined an initial challenge focused on improving both agent and customer experience without compromising operational efficiency. The guiding question was: “How might we simplify access to call data for agents without disrupting their workflow, while also automating processes to enhance cost, quality, and service efficiency?” This framing provided initial direction, and we used user-centered research to validate needs, understand the operational context, and refine the problem definition.

Methodology

A user-centered design methodology was applied, combining mixed research, rapid prototyping, and continuous validation. While not strictly Scrum, the team worked in weekly sprints with ongoing stakeholder feedback.

User Research

Insights que movieron el diseño

What we did: live shadowing, semi-structured interviews, metrics analysis and competitive benchmarking.

View more

To gain a deep understanding of user needs, behaviors, and pain points, we applied a mixed-method approach combining qualitative and quantitative techniques. Direct access to the call center and internal company data enabled contextual, hands-on research. The following actions were carried out:

  • Live shadowing
  • Semi-structured interviews
  • Metrics analysis
  • Competitive benchmarking

Unexpected insights emerged, such as the impact of workplace environment on end-user experience and the need for more intuitive call management tools.
